Top Seasons between St. Louis, MO and Brevard County, FL

It's officially been a year since I moved to the midwest. Prior to that, I lived in Florida. Never seen snow, fall leaves, or spring bloom until this year. Here is my review of the seasons between my hometown and St. Louis.

Top Seasons

  1. Spring -- St. Louis, MO

    2. 8/10. Beautiful transition from winter to spring. Nature is illuminating during this time. Trees blooming colors I thought could only exist in my imagination.

  2. Winter -- Brevard County, FL

    3. 7.5/10. It's like fall/spring weather for yall but it only last 3 months tops. Plus lots of random hot days. We've experience several Christmases in shorts. Sun is completely down at 6:00 PM. Things feel right during this time. This is the sunniest time of the year.

  3. Fall -- St. Louis, MO

    4. 7/10. It only last a month. Why? It was a glorious month and the trees are brillant. Nobody told me how fast things change. Monday we had trees full of leaves, and Thursday 75% are completely gone.

  4. Winter -- St. Louis, MO

    5. 6/10. The worst of it is just icy and wet. The best is the glittery snow and snowflakes so detailed its beyond me. Beauty that I could only see on a screen until now. Seeing a deer in snow was surreal. Wearing layers kind of sucks if you have to constantly take them off but at least there's no sweat involved.

  5. Spring -- Brevard County, FL

    6. 4/10. Maybe last 2 weeks and then you'll start sweating profusely. Also lovebug season.

  6. Summer -- St. Louis, MO

    7. 4/10. Summer gets just as hot and humid as Florida. But at least you get a break. Random days where it's kind of cool out is a plus.

  7. Fall -- Brevard County, FL

    8. 3/10 There is no such thing as fall in Central Florida and going south. This screenshot was taken 10/14/2017. This represents the joy of seeing a day with a high in the 70s. It most likely felt like 90 degrees that day BUT with a cool breeze. Also still dealing with hurricane season.

  8. Summer -- Brevard County, FL

    9. 0/10. The moment you walk outside, you can already feel yourself producing sweat in every place of your body. Humidity slaps you in the face. It's an inescapable 9 months of suffering. "Scattered showers" is an everyday thing. It's either a severe thunderstorm that will last for about an hour or 5 mins of random rain showers throughout the day. Hot rain. Not uncommon to see your backyard pouring rain and the front yard dry as can be. It's so hot, I personally hibernate. Hurricanes usually pop up August - October. By far the most uncomfortable season I've ever experienced.

Stay tuned to see what I think next year.
