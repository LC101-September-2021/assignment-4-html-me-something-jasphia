Top Seasons between St. Louis, MO and Brevard County, FL

Top Seasons

  1. Winter -- Brevard County, FL

    2. 8/10. It's like fall/spring weather for yall but it only last 3 months TOPS. Plus lots of random hot days. We've experience several Christmas with 85 degree weather. Sun is completely down at 6:00 PM. Things feel right during this time. This is the sunniest time of the year.

  2. Spring -- St. Louis, MO

    3. 8/10. Beautiful transition from winter to spring. Nature is illuminating during this time. Trees like I've never seen before. Seriously thought that Spring colors were a myth.

  3. Fall -- St. Louis, MO

    4. 7/10. Less a point than spring because why the heck does fall weather only last a month? It was a glorious month and the trees are brillant. Nobody told me how fast things change. Monday we had trees full of leaves, and Thursday 75% are completely gone.

  4. Winter -- St. Louis, MO

    5. 6/10. Where's the snow first of all. The worst of it is just icy and wet. The best is the glittery snow and snowflakes so detailed its beyond me. Seeing a deer in snow was surreal. Wearing layers kind of sucks if you have to constantly take them off but at least there's no sweat involved.

  5. Spring -- Brevard County, FL

    6. 4/10. Maybe last 2 weeks and then you'll start sweating profusely.

  6. Summer -- St. Louis, MO

    7. 4/10. Y'all summers gets just as hot and humid as Florida. But at least y'all get a break. And random days where it's kind of cool out (for me as a FL).

  7. Fall -- Brevard County, FL

    8. 3/10 There is no such thing as fall in Central Florida and going south. This screenshot was taken 10/14/2017. This represents the joy of seeing a day with a high in the 70s. It most likely felt like 90 degrees that day.

  8. Summer -- Brevard County, FL

    9. 0/10. The minute you go outside you feel yourself sweating in areas you don't want to be. Good luck if you have no a/c in your car. Best thing to do is ignore it. It's a hell for 9 months and it always rains. "Scattered showers" is an everyday thing. It's either a severe thunderstorm that will last for less than an hour or 5 mins of random rain showers throughout the day. Not uncommon to see your backyard pouring rain and the front yard dry as can be. It's so hot, I personally hibernate.
